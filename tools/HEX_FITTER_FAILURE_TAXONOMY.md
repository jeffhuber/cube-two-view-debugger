# Hex-fitter failure taxonomy

Diagnostics-only artifact synthesizing two evidence sources:

1. **`_fit_hexagon_to_hull` walkthrough data** on 9 worst corpus pairs
   (sets 17, 21, 30, 31, 44, 47, 57, 58, 61) √ó 2 sides = 18 pairs.
   Generated by `tools/generate_hex_fitter_walkthroughs.py`.

2. **Human visual-feedback labels** from the May 18 overlay-tool review.
   Captured at `tests/fixtures/overlay_visual_labels.json`
   (parsed from the source xlsx by `tools/parse_overlay_visual_labels.py`).

Goal: turn intuition about "geometry fails" into structured per-slot
labels and per-pair quantitative data so future fixes can target
specific failure modes rather than tuning thresholds blind.

## Headline finding

**12 of 18 (67%) worst-pair hexagons are degenerate** ‚ÄĒ at least one
hexagon edge is < 20 px long, meaning two adjacent hexagon vertices
collapsed onto roughly the same image point. The 9 worst-pair
degeneracy rate matches the previously-surveyed whole-corpus rate
(63%) ‚ÄĒ the corpus-wide bottleneck IS the same one that surfaces in
the worst pairs.

## Collapse-position distribution

Across the 12 degenerate hexagons (some pairs have multiple collapses):

| collapse position | meaning | count |
|---|---|---|
| `h4-h5` | upper-left + left-middle cube corners merge | 8 |
| `h0-h1` | top + upper-right cube corners merge | 5 |
| `h2-h3` | right-middle + bottom cube corners merge | 4 |
| **total collapses** | | **17 across 12 pairs** |

The `h4-h5` position dominates because the corpus has many cubes
slightly yawed to the right (the user's natural iPhone handhold),
which compresses the cube's left side in image space ‚ÄĒ the
upper-left and left-middle corners project close together.

Sets 21 A and 47 A are the **extreme degeneracy** cases (all 3 pairs
collapsed simultaneously) ‚ÄĒ the silhouette projects as a triangle,
not a hexagon. The cube is yawed enough that **only 3 distinct cube
corners are visible on the hull**; the other 3 cube corners are
"interior" ‚ÄĒ they meet at the cube-center vertex INSIDE the
silhouette.

## Structural failure mode (the key insight)

The angular-sector algorithm in `_fit_hexagon_to_hull`
(`tools/propose_geometry_labels.py:308`) divides the hull into 6
sectors of 60¬į each starting at the topmost vertex (anchor), then
picks the farthest-from-centroid vertex within each sector.

**Why this fails on yawed cubes:** when 2 cube corners fall in the
same 60¬į angular sector (because the projection compresses them),
only ONE is picked as that sector's winner. The adjacent sector
then picks **the same cube corner** (it's also the farthest hull
vertex in that direction). Two adjacent hexagon vertices end up at
the same cube corner ‚Üí collapsed edge.

**Why a better hull-based fitter can't fully solve this** ‚ÄĒ see
panel 7 of any walkthrough. Visvalingam-Whyatt eliminates the
degenerate-edge problem (all 18 pairs produce min_edge ‚Č• 196 px),
BUT it picks "the 3 real cube corners + 3 most-prominent hull
bulges between them." Those 3 bulges are NOT cube corners ‚ÄĒ they're
ear-of-the-silhouette bumps where the hexagon vertices SHOULD be.

For yawed cubes, the missing 3 hexagon vertices are at the
**cube-center vertex and its immediate neighbors** ‚ÄĒ geometrically
INSIDE the silhouette, where the 3 visible cube faces meet. No
hull-based algorithm can find them; the information isn't on the
hull.

**Pattern**: the pairs with the worst human verdicts (most "bad"
cells) AND the most slot/src mismatches ALSO have the most extreme
hexagon degeneracy. The human-perceived failure modes (multi-face
spans, wrong source-face attribution) trace back to the same
upstream problem ‚ÄĒ the hexagon doesn't describe the cube's actual
6-corner geometry, so downstream "slot 'L' actually got a B-face
grid" decisions are pre-poisoned.

## What this taxonomy implies for next signals

The walkthrough + label data points at **two complementary directions**:

### Direction 1: better h1/h3/h5 source than the hull

The structural finding (the inner 3 cube corners aren't on the hull)
means hull-based fitter improvements are bounded. To find h1/h3/h5,
candidate signals are:

- **Interior bezel-line detection.** The cube's internal face
  boundaries cast dark lines visible in the photo even when not on
  the silhouette. Edge detection inside the silhouette + 3-line
  Hough or RANSAC could find the cube-center vertex.
- **Per-sticker segmentation.** SAM2 AMG (proven viable in PR #171
  research) reliably finds 27 stickers per cube ‚ÄĒ including
  stickers that bracket the inner cube corners.
- **Learned vertex prediction.** A small CNN that sees the full
  image (not just the hull) and predicts 6 hexagon-vertex
  coordinates. Earlier face-quad regressor underperformed (37%
  pass) on small data; a vertex-specific model with leave-one-set-out
  validation could improve.

### Direction 2: catch wrong-source-face attribution before it
        propagates downstream

The slot/src mismatch pattern in the May 18 labels (4 of 5 pairs
have ‚Č•2 mismatches) is what poisons rectification. Candidate signals
that don't require fixing the hexagon:

- **Cell-internal color discontinuity.** A grid cell crossing a
  face boundary has high color variance INSIDE the cell (sticker A
  + bezel + sticker B). Previously tried this session as a
  rejection gate (didn't discriminate cleanly); Codex recommends
  shipping as a probe diagnostic first, mine for zero-FP candidates
  over many sweeps.
- **Per-grid color-multiset coherence.** A clean single-face grid's
  9 stickers should match a known face's typical color distribution
  (e.g., U face on side A has known stickers per GT). A multi-face
  span produces an off-distribution multiset.
- **Cross-face-edge consistency.** Adjacent face quads should share
  a corner (the cube-center vertex). If proposer's F quad has no
  corner near R quad's corner, that's a slot/src mismatch signal.

## Reproducing this artifact

```bash
# 1. Parse the xlsx into structured labels
.venv/bin/python tools/parse_overlay_visual_labels.py

# 2. Render walkthroughs for the 9 worst pairs
.venv/bin/python tools/generate_hex_fitter_walkthroughs.py \
    --sets 17 21 30 31 44 47 57 58 61 \
    --out /tmp/hex_fitter_walkthroughs
```

The walkthroughs are not committed to the repo (large PNGs); the
script + this taxonomy doc + the JSON labels are the durable
artifacts.
